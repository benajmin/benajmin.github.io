I Accidentally Volunteered at a Robotics Competition

March 2018

When the organiser of UWRi3D asked me to help setup for the First Robotics Competition, I was more than happy to help out. I thought I'd go for the morning, set up the field, maybe get some free food and leave in time for CS class. But boy was I wrong.

The start of CS came and passed without me even noticing. I was enjoying myself so much I didn't want to leave. Once the field was setup and teams started to arrive I stayed around, hoping there was more I could help with. I was told to hang out at pit admin, in case I was needed for anything else. While I was at pit admin there was an announcement that needed to be made, and since no one else wanted to do it, I volunteered.

Before I knew it I was a volunteer for the whole weekend. The volunteer coordinator like my announcer voice, and suddenly I had a name tag, a t-shirt and later on even a walkie talkie. I announced upcoming matches all weekend, and I had an amazing time doing it.

I'd never been to an FRC event before. My highschool only started a team after I left, so it was all a new and exciting experience. It was amazing to see the teams working together, to see the massive robots they had built in just six weeks.

But even more amazing was how the teams helped each other. I remember announcing a part request for a team, and within minutes seeing at least 5 different teams running over to help. FIRST's focus on gracious proffessionalism has created an amazing environment of teamwork and cooperation. An environment that I'm proud to now be a part of.
